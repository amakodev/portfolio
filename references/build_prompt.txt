Build an interactive recruiter-facing portfolio journey from the uploaded spreadsheet:

“github_repo_portfolio_130_repos(1).xlsx”

This must not be a spreadsheet viewer.

The goal is to turn the spreadsheet into a premium, recruiter-friendly portfolio experience that proves execution ability, product thinking, technical range, and systems-oriented building.

The final experience should help a recruiter quickly understand:

1. What kind of builder this is.
2. What roles this portfolio supports.
3. What the strongest projects are.
4. What technical and product skills are evidenced.
5. What projects are public, private, prototype, MVP, active, archived, or production-ready.
6. Which projects have screenshots or demo links.
7. Why this builder is desirable in the market.
8. Why the work is not random or scattered.
9. How the portfolio translates into hiring value.

The main positioning should be:

“Not just projects. Evidence of how I build systems.”

Do not lead with “130 repositories.”
Lead with the pattern those repositories reveal.

Use the 130 repositories as supporting evidence, not as the headline value.
